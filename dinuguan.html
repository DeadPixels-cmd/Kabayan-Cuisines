Dinuguan

(3 - 5 serving)

Ingredients

  • 3 cups pork blood
  • 3 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil (canola, sunflower, corn, or any)
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and chopped
  • 1 bulb garlic, peeled and minced I thumb-size ginger (about 1 tbsp), peeled and minced
  • 3 pcs bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce(patis)
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 finger chilies (siling haba)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup pork lean meat
  • 1/4 cup pork intestine
  • 1/4 cup pork flower
Instructions
1
In a bowl, combine pig's blood and about 2 tablespoons of vinegar. Stir well.
2
In a pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions, garlic, and ginger and cook until lightly browned.
3
Add pork meat and innards and cook, stirring occasionally until lightly browned and crispy.
4
Add black pepper while stirring.
5
Add bay leaf and simmer for 2 minutes.
6
Add little water and reduce heat when pork blood is about to mix.
7
Add pork blood, stirring to disperse and avoid lumps.
8
Add fish sauce, vinegar and salt, and bring to boil. In medium flame, cook uncovered and without stirring for about 30 minutes or until pork is tender.
9
Add long green chilies and simmer for 3 minutes.
10
Remove on flame. Serve hot with rice or puto.
