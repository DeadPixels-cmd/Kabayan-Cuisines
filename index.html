Know the secrets of Philippine Dishes

    About

       The cuisines of more than a hundred ethno-linguistic groups located across the Philippine archipelago make up Filipino cuisine. However, the bulk of popular Filipino foods come from the cuisines of the archipelago's ethnolinguistic groups and tribes, such as the Ilocano, Pangasinan, Kapampangan, Tagalog, Bicolano, Visayan (Cebuano, Hiligaynon, and Waray), Chavacano, and Maranao ethnolinguistic groups. Over many centuries, the style of food preparation and the food associated with it have evolved from Austronesian origins (shared with Malaysian and Indonesian cuisines) to a mixed cuisine of Chinese, Spanish, and American influences, in line with the major waves of influence that have enriched the archipelago's cultures, as well as others adapted to indigenous ingredients and the local palate."
